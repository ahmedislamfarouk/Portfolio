# AI-Based Renal Rejection Grade Classification

### 🏆 3rd Place | University of Louisville Research Project (R!L Competition)

A non-invasive approach to classify renal rejection grades in kidney transplant patients. Developed during my research internship at the University of Louisville bioengineering labs.

## 🛠️ Key Contributions

- Integrated **imaging, genomic, and clinical data** to classify renal rejection into grades.
- Compared multiple classification combinations to optimize accuracy.
- Implemented diverse machine learning algorithms including **Random Forest, SVM, Neural Networks, XGBoost, and CatBoost**.
- Performed extensive cross-validation and hyperparameter tuning.

## 🔬 Technologies Used

- **Python**: Core development language.
- **AI Libraries**: TensorFlow, Scikit-learn, XGBoost, CatBoost.
- **Data Processing**: Pandas, NumPy, OpenCV (for medical imaging).
- **Domain**: Medical AI, Bioinformatics.

- Presented a research poster summarizing the findings and methodology.
